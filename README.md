## 1. Executive Summary
This Request for Information (RFI) pertains to an ongoing forensic investigation into an international fraud ring. Initial analysis indicates a highly structured **"sweetheart scam"** (Romance Fraud) operation involving over **$1.248M** in illicit wire transfers. 

The primary objective of this inquiry is to gather specific account metadata to correlate identified threat actors, map the network's financial infrastructure, and facilitate the legal freezing of remaining stolen assets.

---

## 2. Specific Intelligence Requirements (SIRs)
To bridge current knowledge gaps, the following data points are required for all accounts associated with this investigation:

* **Account Attribution:** Full legal names, registered physical addresses, and all secondary contact methods associated with the recipient accounts.
* **Transaction Telemetry:** A comprehensive **90-day history** of all incoming and outgoing transfers to identify downstream "money mule" layers.
* **Access Metadata:** IP addresses, device identifiers, and timestamps for the last **10 successful logins** to determine geographic origin.

---

## 3. Social Engineering Indicators (TTPs)
In addition to financial tracing, this investigation identified several consistent **Tactics, Techniques, and Procedures (TTPs)** used by the threat actors to manipulate victims:

* **Artificial Urgency:** Creating "emergency" financial needs (e.g., medical crises, legal fees) to bypass a victim's logical skepticism.
* **Isolation Tactics:** Gradually discouraging victims from discussing the relationship with family members or financial advisors.
* **Platform Migration:** Quickly moving the victim from secure dating apps to encrypted messaging platforms (WhatsApp/Telegram) to avoid platform-based fraud detection.
* **Scripted Grooming:** The use of repetitive, high-frequency communication to build deep psychological dependency before the first financial "ask."
